World Cup Pulse

Every match becomes a living portrait. Two cloth blankets — one per team — meet at a moving front: the possession tide that shows who is camped in whose half, minute by minute. Hills of relief rise wherever the danger is, and a goal floods the whole pitch in the scorer's colour while the sky leans to the leader. Along the bottom, a pulse beats out the momentum of the game like a seismograph. The aim is functional art: honest football structure made instantly readable — and beautiful.
