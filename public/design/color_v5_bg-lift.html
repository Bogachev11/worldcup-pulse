Background v5 — the "too black" study

Same France/Senegal blankets on six base tones. Pure black (#000) and the current #05070d read as a harsh void — no atmosphere, the cloth edge is a hard cut. Premium data-art (Bremer/Visual Cinnamon, NYT, The Pudding) almost never sits on true black; it uses a lifted near-neutral (L≈8–14) often with a hint of hue and a faint radial vignette-in-reverse so the centre glows a touch above the corners.

Recommendation. Move off #05070d to a lifted deep plum-navy around #101625 at centre easing to #0b0f18 at the edges (a soft reverse-vignette), optionally with the faintest chroma-gated score-tint already in the engine. This keeps the blankets the unquestioned star, adds the "gallery" atmosphere the piece wants, and gives dark team colours (France navy, Germany grey) an edge to sit against instead of merging into a black hole. Keep text off pure white — use #e9e7f4.
