Colour approach v4 — Flag-stripe / pattern fills

The blanket is filled with the country's flag banding (or a per-team texture) rather than one flat colour. Pattern is a second channel beyond hue — so even when two teams share a colour, their weave differs. This is the redundant-encoding fix data-viz uses for colourblind safety, applied for football identity.

How it reads. Pattern adds a second identity channel, so the red/red clash finally resolves: Canada's white flag-band reads differently from Morocco's green pentagram weave even though the base hue matches. It's also the colourblind-safe route (colour + texture = redundant encoding). Risk: on a soft, undulating 3D cloth with moving xG relief, fine stripes can turn to visual noise / moiré and fight the "calm gallery" north star — patterns must be broad and low-contrast, essentially a whisper.

PRO: only option that truly solves clashes; colourblind-safe; richest national read. CON: most complex to render on cloth; easy to over-do into busyness; can undercut the minimal, premium feel.
