Each blanket takes the country's flag primary (France flag-blue #0055A4, Senegal flag-green #00853F, Canada flag-red #FF0000, Morocco flag-red #C1272D). Flags are the most universally recognised national signal — a broad audience reads "France = bleu" without being a fan.

How it reads. Flag colours are the most legible national signal to a layperson — better recall than kit or brand colours. FRA/SEN is crisp. But flags still clash: Canada and Morocco are both flag-red, so the clash problem is not solved by switching source — a red/red pair stays red/red. Many flags also share a small palette (red, white, green, blue) so collisions are common.

PRO: instant national read for a broad audience; culturally "correct". CON: still clashes; white-flag countries (much of the field) give no colour at all against near-black; needs the same de-clash logic as v1.
